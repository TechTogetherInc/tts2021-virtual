Tracks (Challenges)

Stereotype Smasher Hack


We can all name stereotypes in the tech industry which have made us or others feel intimidated to participate. Create a hack that helps break the stereotypes in this industry and make it a more inclusive and welcoming place.

Best Meme-Worthy Hack


It’s totally okay to let loose once in a while. We want to recognize the hacker who created the most wacky hack. The more roundabout and out-right silly – the better.

Inclusive Hack


Learning to Code should not be limited by gender, race, sexuality, or nationality. Build a solution that creates greater access to computer science for students around the globe, especially those from marginalized communities.

Most Unconventional Hack


You are not bound to society's expectations! Create a hack which challenges the way we view the world or an “unconventional hack” that can use technology to help solve social problems in a way we have never seen before.

Best Connectivity Hack


The pandemic has affected many aspects of our lives and one of them is our relationships with the people around us. Create a hack that helps improve connectivity and communication between people whether it is our loved ones or our co-workers/peers.

FAQs

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where individuals or teams work together to build a technology-based solution to a problem in a short period of time (24-36 hours). With the help of workshops and mentors, you can build an application, a website, a robot — there are endless possibilities!

Who is eligible to participate?

Hackers

We are committed to providing people of marginalized genders with opportunities to explore the tech industry in an inspiring, encouraging, and energizing environment. At TechTogether Seattle, we give each hackers regardless of age or experience level the unique opportunity to gain and develop their skill-sets, build a professional network, and be part of this empowering community.

Non-Hackers (Mentors, Judges, etc.)

Other ways to get involved include joining as a mentor, judge, workshop host, or panelist (see Get Involved for roles and links) or support us as a sponsor! This is a great opportunity to expand your network, grow, and to share with our budding community!

What if it's my first hackathon?

No worries! We’ve prepared an event dedicated to first-time hackers and an online resource kit to get you fully prepared and ready to hack during the weekend. Our mentors are also available to help guide you through your projects during the hackathon. Additionally, feel free to reach out if you have access needs or questions.

Is there a cost to attend?

TechTogether Seattle Hackathon is free to attend!

How do teams work?

Teams can consist up to 4 hackers. You can apply with or without a team, and there will be team-forming event(s) prior to our hackathon to meet others and form your team.

What if I don't like coding?

That's completely ok! Hackers have many roles besides coding including project management and design! Your team will need people to help ideate, design, prototype, and pitch your project in order to bring it to life, so your unique skills will definitely be an asset!

Where do I submit my project?

There will be a Devpost page where you and your team will submit your project and view the judging criterias. At the end of the hackathon, we will be announcing the winners on this page as well! (You can also view the other projects here.)

Will be there swag and prizes?

We’ll be giving out prizes throughout the hackathon, especially for winners of the various tracks. Outside of it, we’ll also be hosting raffles throughout the workshops. Attendee swag this year will be swapped for local charity donations instead.

How will mentoring work?

Mentoring will all be done through our Discord. If you or your team want to connect with a mentor, fill out the form on the Discord channel and then hop onto our Mentorship Zoom hangout to talk with your assigned mentor.

Where do I attend workshops, etc?

All workshops, activities, and events will be taking place online through Zoom this year. We will share meeting links via Discord. We’ll also be sharing workshop recordings via a Google Drive folder through Discord after each event. So if you can’t make it to one of the hackathon's workshops, we’ve got you covered! Although we highly recommend joining real-time, if possible, for opportunities to network and Q&A.

Where is my Discord invite?

After registering on Eventbrite, you will be emailed a link to join the Discord 1- 2 days before the hackathon. Make sure to check your spam folder for all event emails.

What if I have access needs?

TechTogether Seattle was built with inclusion in mind, and although we have aimed to create that environment in advance, we understand that a wide range access needs exist and lack of support may prevent you from fully engaging in the event. Please allow us to help you the best we can by contacting us in advance through this link.
